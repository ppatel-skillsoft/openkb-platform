# Introduction to Stellar Classification

Stars are classified by their physical properties using the Hertzsprung-Russell diagram,
which plots luminosity against surface temperature.

## Main Sequence Stars

The main sequence is the longest phase of a star's life, during which hydrogen fusion
occurs in the core. Our Sun is a main sequence star of spectral type G.

## Red Giants

When a main sequence star exhausts its hydrogen supply, the core contracts and the outer
layers expand dramatically — forming a red giant. Red giants are significantly cooler
on the surface but vastly larger in radius.

## Stellar Evolution Cycles

After the red giant phase, lower-mass stars eject their outer shells as a planetary nebula,
leaving behind a white dwarf. Higher-mass stars undergo supernovae, producing neutron stars
or black holes.

## Key Concepts

- Hertzsprung-Russell diagram: temperature vs luminosity classification tool
- Main sequence: hydrogen-fusing phase (90% of stellar lifetime)
- Red giant: post-main-sequence expansion phase
- Planetary nebula: ejected outer shell of a dying star
- Stellar nucleosynthesis: heavy elements forged in stellar cores
