# Introduction to Plant Cell Biology

Plants are living organisms that produce energy through photosynthesis, a process
confined to specialised organelles called chloroplasts.

## Chloroplasts

Chloroplasts are double-membrane organelles found in plant cells. They contain
chlorophyll pigments that absorb sunlight. The chloroplast stroma is where the
Calvin cycle converts carbon dioxide into glucose.

## Photosynthesis

Photosynthesis is the conversion of light energy into chemical energy stored as sugar.
The light-dependent reactions occur in the thylakoid membranes; the Calvin cycle
(light-independent reactions) occurs in the stroma.

## Gas Exchange via Stomata

Carbon dioxide enters leaves through microscopic pores called stomata. Guard cells
control stomatal opening and closing in response to light and water availability.
Oxygen produced by photosynthesis exits the leaf through the same stomata.

## Transport Systems

- Xylem: transports water and dissolved minerals from roots to leaves (upward flow)
- Phloem: distributes sugars produced in leaves to the rest of the plant (bidirectional)

## Key Concepts

- Chloroplast: site of photosynthesis; contains chlorophyll
- Photosynthesis: light energy → glucose + oxygen
- Stomata: gas exchange pores regulated by guard cells
- Xylem / Phloem: vascular transport tissues
- Calvin cycle: CO2 fixation pathway in the chloroplast stroma
